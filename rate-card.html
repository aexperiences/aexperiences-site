What we charge, and why.

Everything we sell runs on one pricing idea. Learn the idea and you can quote any vertical without looking anything up.

The whole model, in one line
One-time build  +  flat monthly

The build fee is us taking the hub and making it theirs — their brand, their domain, their workflow, their data moved in. Paid once, up front.
The monthly is hosting, support, updates and the AI running inside it. Never a per-seat fee, never a per-transaction cut. Their team can grow without the price moving.

The seven hubs

Same software underneath, shaped for a different trade. Three tiers each — small shop, established firm, multi-location. Start them where they are; moving up a tier later is a conversation, not a migration.

The App Shop

A separate, much smaller sale — and a good door-opener for someone not ready for a hub.

Invent an App

$99 per app

Half up front, half when it ships. Roughly three days. Standard app-shop scope — if they want it customized beyond that, it is quoted separately and it is not $99.

  • They get lifetime access to the app they paid for.
  • The app then lists in the shop, and other buyers pay a subscription.
  • We keep that subscription revenue.

What that means for the pitch

They are not buying a custom build at a custom price. They are paying to move their idea to the front of a line, and getting a real app out of it.

It is the cheapest way for a skeptical prospect to find out whether we actually deliver — which is usually the real objection behind a stalled hub deal.

The five questions you will actually get

Short answers. Say them plainly and move on.

"Why is there a build fee at all?"
Because they are not renting a template — we rebuild the hub around how their business actually runs, on their brand and their domain. That work happens once, so it is billed once. The monthly stays flat afterward.
"That monthly is more than [competitor]."
Compare the whole stack, not one line. Most firms are paying four or five separate subscriptions plus per-seat fees. Ours is one number that does not move when they hire.
"Can we start smaller?"
Yes — start at the entry tier. Moving up later is a settings change and a new monthly, not a rebuild. Never discount the build fee to close; step them down a tier instead.
"Can we see it first?"
Yes, and this is our best move. Send them the live hub for their trade from the App Shop. It is real working software, not a slide deck — walking them through it beats any brochure.
"Can you do it cheaper?"
Pricing changes are not yours or mine to give — bring it back to us and we will decide together. Discounting on the call is how the whole card loses its meaning.
