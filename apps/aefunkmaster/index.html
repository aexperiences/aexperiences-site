Plain-English owner's notes (what everything does)

Play starts the drum groove and the loop grid. BPM is the speed. Click is a metronome.

Bass / Middle / Sparkle on each lane are tone controls: Bass = boom, Middle = body, Sparkle = brightness. How loud is the fader. Left–Right places it in the room.

Looper: loops line up to whole bars while Play is on, so everything stays in time. Stop a slot with Play/Stop on that slot; Clear wipes it. ½ speed drops a loop an octave into tape-slow territory; Reverse flips it backwards.

Groove A / Groove B are two drum patterns. Chain A→B plays them back to back, switching every bar — verse and chorus for your beat.

Echo repeats a sound in time with the tempo. Room puts it in a space. A little goes a long way.

The studio remembers your setup on this device — grooves, the desk, wax. Loops and the mic are live, so they start fresh each visit.

Put it on wax gently saturates and glues the whole mix — the "finished record" sound. Dust adds a quiet vinyl crackle underneath.

Record your song captures exactly what you hear (all lanes + wax). Downloads as WAV — drop it anywhere.

Everything runs on your device. Nothing is uploaded, no account, nothing collected.
